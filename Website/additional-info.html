Global Internet User Stats

According to the estimates of Freedom House, here are some important statistics regarding the 3.7 billion people with internet access:
71% live in countries where information and communications technology users were arrested or imprisoned for posting political, social, or religious content.
55% live in countries with blocked political, social, or religious content.
48% live in countries where there have been attacks or murders related to online activities.
47% live in countries where the authorities have increased their surveillance regarding user rights to privacy.
47% live in countries where social media or messaging platforms have been blocked temporarily or permanently.
42% live in countries where the government (often for political reasons) has disconnected internet or mobile networks.


Largest Five-Year Declines

Of the 65 countries in question, the following five underwent the most significant decrease in internet freedom within a five year time span (2014-2018):

Cambodia: Amendments made in 2015 and 2018 to the Telecommunications Law and penal code resulted in an increase in arrests for political commentary on social media and news platforms.

Venezuela: Internet access was reduced for citizens in relation to the country's economic crisis. Critical media sites are blocked, and political commentary is penalized.

Ukraine: Related to the conflict with separatists (Russian-backed) and the Kremlin information war.

Egypt: Digital activism often results in jail-time, and lengthy prison terms are sentenced for minor offenses. They also experienced a dramatic escalation in censorship from 2015 to 2018.

Turkey: Thousands of citizens arrested for criticizing the government. Social media companies are censoring even non-violent political commentary as a cost of doing business in Turkey.


These statistics are collected from Freedom of the Net 2018: The Rise of Digital Authoritarianism by Adrian Shahbaz.
